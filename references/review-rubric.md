# Review Rubric

Use this rubric for draft review and revision planning.

## Severity

- `Critical`: invalidates the paper's main story, core claim, or technical correctness
- `Important`: materially weakens confidence, completeness, or fairness
- `Minor`: presentation, clarity, wording, or lower-impact completeness issue

## Review Dimensions

### 1. Problem And Contribution

Check:
- Is the problem clearly defined?
- Is the claimed contribution specific?
- Are contributions actually research contributions rather than implementation tasks?

### 2. Novelty And Positioning

Check:
- Are nearest related works identified?
- Is the delta to prior work concrete?
- Is the claimed novelty proportional to the actual difference?

### 3. Method Validity

Check:
- Are assumptions stated?
- Is the method internally coherent?
- Are there missing details that prevent understanding or reproduction?

### 4. Evidence Sufficiency

Check:
- Does each main claim have supporting evidence?
- Are negative results or limitations hidden?
- Are claims too broad for the presented data?

### 5. Experimental Design

Check:
- Are baselines relevant and fairly configured?
- Are metrics appropriate?
- Are ablations or sensitivity studies needed?
- Are qualitative examples used honestly?

### 6. Presentation

Check:
- Does each section advance the story?
- Are figures and tables pulling their weight?
- Is the writing precise rather than inflated?

## Output Rule

Findings first. Summaries second.
