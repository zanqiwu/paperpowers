# Figure Patterns

Use this file to pick figures that clarify the paper's argument.

## Method Overview Figure

Use when:
- the paper introduces a pipeline or architecture

Must show:
- inputs
- main transformation stages
- what is new
- outputs

Avoid:
- box-and-arrow art that hides the contribution

## Problem Setup Figure

Use when:
- the task or setting is unfamiliar
- assumptions matter

Must show:
- entities
- data flow
- constraints or observations

## Positioning Or Taxonomy Figure

Use when:
- the literature space is crowded
- your method occupies a specific design point

Must show:
- meaningful axes
- where representative prior works sit
- where your method differs

## Ablation Summary Figure

Use when:
- several controlled comparisons support one message

Must show:
- the design choices compared
- magnitude and direction of effect

Avoid:
- repeating the same information already clear in a table

## Qualitative Figure

Use when:
- human-interpretable behavior matters

Must show:
- why the example is representative or diagnostic
- what the reader should notice

Avoid:
- cherry-picked examples without stated purpose

## Efficiency Tradeoff Figure

Use when:
- speed, memory, or cost is part of the contribution

Must show:
- the tradeoff axis
- where baselines sit
- what regime your method improves
