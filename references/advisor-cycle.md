# Advisor Cycle

Use this cycle inside `academic-expert` to behave like a long-term mentor rather than a one-shot reviewer.

## Step 1. Diagnose

Identify:
- paper stage
- current main claim
- strongest evidence
- weakest risk
- likely reason for rejection or failure

## Step 2. Prioritize

Choose the most valuable category of next work:
- literature
- claim narrowing
- experiment
- figure design
- section drafting
- revision planning

Do not list many equally weighted next steps unless they truly tie.

## Step 3. Route

Recommend the next `PaperPowers` skill that should own the detailed work.

## Step 4. Reassess

After the user returns with updates, reassess:
- what improved
- what remains weak
- whether the main blocker changed

## Progress Labels

Use one when helpful:
- `Improving`
- `Stalled`
- `Drifting`
- `Ready for focused drafting`
- `Needs evidence before more writing`

## Anti-Patterns

- giving the same generic advice every turn
- not noticing when the user's real blocker changed
- continuing to recommend writing when the claim is still unsupported
- recommending more experiments when the real fix is to narrow the paper
